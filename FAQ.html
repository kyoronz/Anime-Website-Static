Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

A: Four Leaves Anime was created as a news source that focuses on the medium of Japanese Animation(anime) and Japanese Comics (manga). Four Leaves Anime was founded by 4 members, who are Fong Suo Xuan, Law Yong Hui, Ng Pei Jia and Hoo Jun Yi in 2016, and has since lead the online anime news medium with a tradition of strong unbiased coverage of major breaking news. With in-depth coverage, analysis and commentary to provide insight into the world of anime and manga. Our goal is to make the latest, most comprehensive Anime news available to new fans and veteran Otaku alike.

A: Anime is an English name for the style of art and animation developed by (but no longer exclusively produced by) the Japanese. The word "anime" is just the Japanese word for animation (of any kind), and it is pronounced "Annie-May" (both like the English names). Other occasionally used words are Japanimation, Japanime, and manga (which is the Japanese word for comic books and animation in general).

A: An otaku is a term used in Japan for someone who is obsessed with something. Basically, a nerd. There are car otaku, A/V otaku, etc. The term comes from the word "otaku" which means "you" (see IIe). It is a very respectful way to refer to someone, and as Gilles Poitras points out, for young socially inept boys, such a safe way of speaking to others is not unusual. Anyway, it can be very insulting to call someone an otaku, since it can be derogatory in Japan.
